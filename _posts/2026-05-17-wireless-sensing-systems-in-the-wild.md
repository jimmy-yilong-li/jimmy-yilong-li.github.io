My wireless sensing work focuses on systems that survive real deployment
conditions: movement, blockage, multipath, synchronization limits, and noisy
hardware. MEDUSA and Gemini represent this line of work from two directions.
MEDUSA uses distributed UWB MIMO radar for robust biometric sensing, while
Gemini explores full-fledged sensing over mmWave communication radios.

The common thread is full-stack system design. The sensing problem is not only
an algorithm problem; it requires RF hardware choices, synchronization, embedded
software, signal processing, and learning models that can absorb real-world
variation.
